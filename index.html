Our passion led us here

LTCA's Commitment

Our Mission

At Learning Tree Christian Academy we purpose to provide a Christian school with a Biblical worldview that enhances spiritual growth for our students and their families, while maintaining a peaceful, safe environment for students, and continuously promoting academic excellence.

Our Vision

Recognizing that early experiences have profound effects on development and learning, Learning Tree Christian Academy sets as its vision a sincere effort to assist parents in providing a positive learning environment with opportunities for personal growth, helping their children build strong character traits that will serve them well in their youth as well as help them become responsible citizens as adults, contributing to the needs of our local, diverse community, state of Mississippi, and extending nationally and internationally as these children grow to make a sincere impact in our world.

Our Philosophy

Learning Tree Christian Academy focuses on the overall development of the whole person including social, emotional, physical, spiritual, creative, cognitive, and language development through a combination of student-led and teacher-directed learning activities. We meet these needs through a loving, Christian, and home-type environment. Although Learning Tree is a Christian-based program, there is no particular denominational influence. Learning Tree also prioritizes safety, cleanliness, and health.

Developmentally-appropriate educational opportunities are provided daily, and we believe the children's enjoyment and learning success in early childhood and elementary school will enhance their desire to continuously learn in the future. Because we know that all children learn differently and at their own pace, LTCA strives to offer differentiated instruction to meet the student's individual learning needs. Since we believe young children learn through play and that play is an important vehicle for developing self-regulation and promotion language, cognition, and social competence, LTCA offers both indoor and outdoor fun opportunities that encourage "hands on" learning. Because we believe experience in the arts is an important part of a person's development, we offer art and music daily.

Our Goals

We want our children at LTCA to thrive in a safe, healthy, loving environment. We want to encourage our children at LTCA to develop a passion for learning that will last a lifetime. We want to assist our children at LTCA to develop at age-appropriate rates in the areas of social and emotional, cognitive, and physical and language development, as determined through Ages and Stages questionnaires (ASQ-3) and assessments.

We want our children's families to feel welcome and an important valued part of our Learning Tree family and plan to get to know them better through daily conversations, scheduled meetings, and planned family events. We want our LTCA parents to meet with our administrator, directors, and teachers regularly to help us better plan for and meet the individual needs of each student including any cultural needs, while assisting with executing and evaluating any assessments. We want to provide opportunities for parents to volunteer in their child's classroom and at school-wide events, knowing children benefit from involvement of their parents, their first and best teachers.

Academics

A Curriculum Proven to Work

All Grades from Start to Finish

Learning Tree Christian Academy utilizes the Abeka curriculum for all classes and teach from a traditional Christian worldview. We have MAIS certified teachers in all classes. LTCA uses quality literature, hands-on learning centers including STEM and technology, music, and art. In learning about God's creation, LTCA features monthly wildlife visits from Freedom Ranch Outreach Education.

  • Abeka curriculum
  • Christian Worldview
  • MAIS Certified Teachers
  • Stem Lab
  • Computer Lab
  • Music, Art and Bible classes
  • Wildlife visits

K3

Learning Tree Christian Academy uses K-3 Abeka curriculum, fun learning centers, music, and art. Our K-3 students enjoy visits from Freedom Ranch. Although daily activities will vary somewhat from time to time, each classroom does follow a planned schedule. Activities include center time educational fun, large group circle time, Abeka school-work time and STEM lab featuring music, art, tech lab, science and robotics, and fitness. During large group time, each teacher welcomes the children, introduces our topics of learning for the day, and teaches activities that can be presented well in a large group setting. The teacher then utilizes small group opportunities to provide one-on-one instruction, increasing opportunities to provide differentiated instruction and help each child learn concepts. The teacher uses Abeka curriculum to present other learning activities such as recognizing and writing letters, learning the short and long vowel sounds, helping the students begin to read, forming numbers and counting objects, and learning colors and basic shapes.

K4 through Grade 3

Our K-4 through Grade 3 children learn to read and master age-appropriate concepts. The students also learn through self-directed, student-led play. They enjoy centers within the room such as the reading corner and the math and science manipulatives section. They also develop hand-eye-coordination and thinking skills through puzzles and table games. Children especially like using their imagination and learning construction skills with the selection of building blocks and other STEM items. The outdoor playground provides the opportunity for children to exercise, play, learn, and enjoy games. For our younger children, the equipment includes foot-powered cars for motor skills. Balls, parachutes, jump ropes, etc. are available for active play. Children will also enjoy playhouses, as well as outdoor chalk centers for creative play and development of hand-eye coordination skills.

Admissions

Steps to Enrollment

01

Selecting a school is such an important decision, so the first step is scheduling a tour. A tour gives you an opportunity to meet the staff, view the facility, and ask questions.

02

Second, before completing an application you will need to read and agree with the Learning Tree Chrisitan Academy Parent Handbook. The handbook contains information about our policies, holiday schedules, and expections for you and us. Then pray. We want your family to be in God's will on the placement of your child's education.

03

Third, You decided LTCA is the right place for you so now complete the application along with the ACH payment form. At Learning Tree Christian Academy, we do everthing possible to make educating your child as affordable as possible. We have been in your shoes.

04

Fourth, Let us know, you desire to enroll your child as a new student. LTCA's staff will meet with you for final time to discuss enrollment and review completed forms and address remaining questions. If we all agree then we will pray and welcome you all to the the LTCA family.

All time slots with associated tuition prices are available during the summer. We still teach core fundamentals during the camp to keep minds fresh, but in addition lots and lots of fun stuff too.

Payment Processing

Tuiton payments are setup through automatic draft (ACH) utilizing a saving or checking account. A credit card can be used temporarily or on an as needed basis with a 3.5% add-on charge. Weekly payments are submitted each Monday. Monthly submission are setup for submission either on the 1st, 2nd, or 3rd day of the month.

Curriculum Fees

A non-refundable annual registration/curriculum/supply fee of $150 is due upon registration and once a year by May 1st, for all children through K-5 Kindergarten. A nonrefundable annual registration/curriculum/supply fee of $350 is due upon registration and once a year by May 1st for all First - Third Grade students.
